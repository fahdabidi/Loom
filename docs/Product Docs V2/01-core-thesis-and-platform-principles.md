## 1. Product Definition

Loom Communities is an **extensible, AI-buildable operating system for real-world communities**. Anyone
can stand up a community — an HOA, a mosque, a parish, a youth soccer club, a book club, a Buy Nothing
circle — and get a custom app for it, generated by an AI Skill (ChatGPT/Gemini/Claude) or written by a
developer, running on top of stable Loom primitives.

The product is not a single hardcoded community app. It is a platform with a fixed, trustworthy
backend and an open extension layer: Loom provides identity, membership, roles, permissions, spaces,
publishing, events, payments, audit, portability, and safety as durable contracts; **extensions** own
the custom experience (UI, layout, workflows, domain logic); and an AI **Skill** authors those
extensions. Communities are discovered by a friendly handle or QR code, downloaded into the Main Loom
app, and kept up to date automatically.

This is the V2 direction. Loom V1 — an open creator/fan economy protocol — is **not being built out
further**; V2 reuses V1's identity, wallet, receipt/audit, extension-runtime, and portability
foundations and re-points them at communities (see §2.4 and the
[reuse map](./Extensible%20Loom%20Product%20Definition.md#2-what-carries-over-from-loom-v1-reuse-map)).

The core promise to **community owners/admins** is:

You can create a community and get a working, custom app in minutes — built with AI — without writing
backend code, without paying upfront (the backend is free, ad-supported), and without locking your
members' data into one vendor. You can always export and switch.

The core promise to **members** is:

You carry one portable identity across every community you belong to, with one inbox, one wallet, one
connections graph, and clear control over your data and consent — and a custom experience tuned to
each community.

The core promise to **extension builders** is:

You can build a complete community app by composing fixed Loom APIs through declared capabilities,
event-triggered rules, workflows, scheduled jobs, and sandboxed functions — generated by an AI Skill
and validated/certified by Loom — without bypassing identity, consent, payments, audit, or export.

## 2. Scope

This product area defines the foundational rules that every other Loom Communities product area must
respect. It covers:

- The platform thesis and the owner/extension/Skill split.
- The product principles.
- The trust boundary and the effective-permission model.
- The reuse of V1 primitives and backward compatibility.
- The role of manifests, receipts, certification, and portability.
- The free-backend-plus-ads monetization stance and its enforcement.
- The baseline experiences for owners, members, builders, providers, and governance.

It does not define the complete feature set for every downstream area. Those belong in the
area-specific definitions in the registry below.

## 2.2 Baseline Terminology and Conventions

Preserve these conventions unless a later governance decision changes them. They follow the V1
conventions ([Core Thesis §2.2](../Product%20Docs/01-core-thesis-and-platform-principles.md)).

### Naming conventions

- User-facing concepts may use spaced names, such as "Community Card".
- Machine-readable contracts use PascalCase, such as `CommunityExtensionManifest`.
- APIs end in `Api`, such as `CommunityRegistryApi`.
- Persistent ledgers end in `Ledger`, registries in `Registry`, policies in `Policy`, schemas in
  `Schema`, receipts in `Receipt`.

### Core nouns

- **Community** — the portable owner container: members, roles, spaces, data, rules, payments, audit.
  Identified by a stable `communityId` and a friendly, unique **handle**.
- **Space** — a sub-community (team, committee, class, ministry, league, division, book room, facility,
  marketplace, care space). Arbitrary-depth nestable and user-extensible.
- **Member** — a person in a community, keyed to a portable **Passport** identity (`passportId`).
- **Extension** — the installable custom experience package (UI, cards, routes, schemas, rules,
  workflows, jobs, permissions). Identified by `extensionId` and versioned.
- **Skill** — the AI authoring tool that generates/updates extension packages. A builder/compiler, not
  the runtime.
- **App ID** — the builder credential created in the Skill, tied to a Loom Identity.
- **Surface** — a stable mount point the shell exposes to an extension (e.g. `community.home`,
  `space.team`, `payment.dues`, `ad.shell_banner`).
- **Connection** — a Passport-level link between two people, used to invite each other into communities.

### Receipt classes (reused from V1)

- `EconomicReceipt` — affects payment, payout, refund, dues, donation, ad revenue, or settlement.
- `AuditReceipt` — transparency, compliance, dispute resolution, safety review, data access.
- `UtilityFundingReceipt` — measures shared-utility usage (identity, vaults, search) for funding.

### Portability classes (reused from V1)

- **Canonical Portable State** — community identity (`communityId`, handle, `CommunityExtensionManifest`
  references), Passport identity, membership, roles, entitlements, provider pointers.
- **Required Export State** — community content, member records, extension state/schemas, receipts,
  payment history, consent records, audit trail.
- **Optional Export State** — derived analytics, layout preferences, recommendation experiments.
- **Non-portable Provider Runtime State** — caches, infra logs beyond audit requirements.

### Certification states (reused from V1)

`Draft`, `Submitted`, `Certified`, `Limited`, `Suspended`, `Revoked`. Certification applies at the
capability/extension-tier level, not by brand. Extension tiers 0–5 are defined in
[API Reference §2.10](./Extensible%20Loom%20API%20Reference.md#210-extension-certification-tiers).

## 2.3 The trust boundary

> **Loom owns the trusted backend contracts, identity, permissions, payments, audit logs,
> portability, and safety. Extensions own the custom community experience: UI, layout, workflows, and
> domain logic.**

Every extension action is gated by the **intersection** of five permission sources — never a generic
`admin`/`write_all` grant:

```text
Extension requested permissions
∩ Community admin-approved permissions
∩ Member role permissions
∩ Member consent grants
∩ Loom platform safety policy
= effective permission
```

## 2.4 Relationship to Loom V1

Loom Communities is **additive and backward-compatible**. A Community is a new top-level container that
sits **beside** the V1 Creator Channel, not a replacement. The existing channel/fan APIs keep working
unchanged; apps built against them are unaffected. V2 reuses the shared layers:

| V1 primitive | V2 use |
| --- | --- |
| Fan Passport | Portable **member identity** (`passportId`) |
| Fan Wallet / Entitlement Ledger | Community **payments** (dues, donations, tickets, ad-off) |
| Receipt Ledger / Settlement Engine | Community **receipts and settlement** |
| Extension registry/runtime (channel-keyed) | **Community** extension registry/runtime (community-keyed) |
| AI Gateway | `CommunityAiApi` and the **Skill** |
| Migration/export APIs | Community **import/export and provider transfer** |
| Contract-first fake-backend demo harness | The **V2 MVP delivery method** |

## 3. Product Principles

### 3.1 Community Ownership and Portability

The owner owns the canonical community relationship — members, roles, spaces, data, rules, payments,
and audit. A provider may host, an extension may render, a payment provider may process a charge, but
none becomes the owner of the community's identity or portable state. The owner can export and migrate
all of it.

### 3.2 Member Portability and One Identity

A member is not trapped in one community or app. One Passport carries identity, wallet, connections,
entitlements, and consent across every community, with pairwise/scoped relationships to limit
unnecessary cross-community tracking.

### 3.3 Extensions Customize, Loom Stays the Trust Boundary

Extensions can customize behavior, but only through declared capabilities, event-triggered rules,
workflows, scheduled jobs, and sandboxed functions that call Loom APIs. They never bypass identity,
consent, payments, audit, or export.

### 3.4 AI Builds, Loom Validates

The Skill generates a portable extension package; Loom validates and certifies it before install.
Prompts are never authoritative policy; certification and capability checks are.

### 3.5 Free Backend, Honest Ads

The backend is free; revenue is advertising. Ads are inserted into core surfaces two ways — a
shell-rendered top banner and video pre/mid-roll, and **in-stream native ad items** injected via API
into interaction streams — and **cannot be designed out** by an extension or Skill. A member
subscription, or a community-owner payment, turns ads off.

### 3.6 Required Shell Structure

Every extension loads into a shell with a thin top ad banner and a navigation panel that always
exposes **Messages** and **Connections** (renamable). The Messaging and Connections shells are
customizable but always reachable. The ad banner is structurally enforced; the navigation pattern is
enforced by the Skill's generation pattern (lintable at certification).

### 3.7 Capability-Scoped Permissions and Consent

Permissions are scoped capabilities, intersected with admin approval, member roles, consent, and
platform safety. Sensitive data (minor/care/donor) lives in protected-visibility vaults with audited,
redacted access.

### 3.8 Transparent Economics Through Receipts

Payments, dues, donations, refunds, ad impressions, and ad-off purchases produce signed receipts.
Settlement is explainable from receipts and manifests.

### 3.9 Progressive Complexity

An owner can start from a template in minutes; deeper customization (rules, workflows, schemas,
functions) and provider choice become available as the community grows. Members get a simple app first.

### 3.10 Open Governance and Certification

Extension certification, API versioning, conformance, revocation, and dispute handling are governed
neutrally so no single provider, app, or AI vendor captures the network. Open-source reference
implementations exist for core components.

## 5. Primary Actors

### Community Owner / Admin

Creates the community (CommunityID + handle), builds/installs and configures the extension, approves
permissions, manages members, roles, spaces, payments, and moderation.

### Member

Joins communities, uses the custom experiences, messages and connects with others, pays dues/donations,
controls consent and data, and can subscribe to remove ads.

### Extension Builder

Registers with the Skill (App ID), logs in with a Loom Identity, and uses the Skill to generate and
publish extension packages.

### Hosting / Service Provider

Hosts community backends or provides specialized certified services (payments, AI, search, moderation,
ad delivery) through certified APIs; reused from V1.

### Advertiser

Supplies creatives delivered into shell ad surfaces and in-stream ad items, within ad policy.

### Governance / Foundation

Maintains extension certification, conformance, registries, revocation, dispute processes, and
open-source reference implementations.

## 6. Product Experience Requirements

### 6.1 Owner Experience

- Create a community quickly; describe it to the Skill and get a working app.
- Understand that the community and its data belong to the owner.
- Approve extension permissions, surfaces, and rules in plain language.
- Publish, manage members/roles/spaces, collect dues/donations, and moderate.
- Pay to turn off ads on the community surface; see basic analytics.
- Export or migrate the community without support tickets.

### 6.2 Member Experience

- Add a community by handle or QR; land in a working, non-empty app.
- One Passport, one inbox (Messages), one connections graph, one wallet.
- Run the community's headline flows (vote, register, pay, request, RSVP).
- Control consent and data; subscribe to remove ads.
- Always run the latest version of the community's extension.

### 6.3 Builder Experience

- Register, get an App ID, log in with a Loom Identity.
- Generate an extension with the Skill; validate, certify, and publish it.
- Receive a QR/handle to share; ship updates that members pull automatically.

### 6.4 Provider Experience

- Register and declare capabilities; run conformance tests; get certified.
- Receive signed receipts for services; get paid through settlement. (Reused from V1.)

### 6.5 Governance Experience

- Validate and certify extension packages; assign tiers.
- Revoke/suspend; resolve disputes; publish certification scope and incidents.

## 7. User Stories

### Story 1: Owner builds and publishes a community with the Skill

As a community owner, I want to describe my community to the Skill and publish a working app, so that
members can join without me writing backend code.

End state: an App ID and Loom Identity exist; a Community (CommunityID + handle) exists; the Skill
generated an extension package; Loom validated and certified it; publish returned a QR code; the
community card is live.

Interacting areas: Owner Experience, Extensions, AI/Skill, Builder Ecosystem, Governance.

### Story 2: Member adds a community by QR and runs the headline flow

As a member, I want to scan a QR code (or search a handle) to add a community and immediately do
something useful, so my first session has value.

End state: the extension downloaded locally; permissions approved; the member completed the headline
flow (vote / register / donate / request); an audit event/receipt was recorded.

Interacting areas: Member Experience, App Shell, Discovery/Connections, Payments.

### Story 3: One identity across many communities

As a member of several communities, I want one identity, inbox, wallet, and connections graph, so I
don't rebuild myself everywhere.

End state: one Passport participates in multiple communities; Messages and Connections are unified;
the wallet is shared; V1 channel surfaces still work.

Interacting areas: Identity, Member Experience, Data Rights, App Shell.

### Story 4: Owner collects dues and a member pays

As a treasurer, I want to generate dues and collect payment with receipts, so the community's finances
are auditable.

End state: a dues plan and invoices exist; a member paid; a receipt issued; an audit event recorded;
a rule marked the invoice paid.

Interacting areas: Payments, Extensions, Trust/Safety.

### Story 5: Sensitive request stays private

As a member, I want to submit a confidential care request visible only to the care team, so my privacy
is preserved.

End state: the request is stored in the protected vault; only the care team can read it; access is
audited; the audit payload is redacted.

Interacting areas: Data Rights, Trust/Safety, Extensions.

### Story 6: Ads fund the free backend; subscription removes them

As a member, I want a free community app supported by ads, and the option to pay to remove them.

End state: the top banner and an in-stream ad item render; the extension can't remove them; a
subscription (or owner payment) turns ads off; ad receipts recorded.

Interacting areas: Monetization, Advertising, App Shell.

### Story 7: Owner exits a provider / exports the community

As an owner, I want to export my community and switch providers, so I'm never locked in.

End state: a migration plan; exported members, content, extension state, receipts; provider pointer
updated; members keep identity and entitlements.

Interacting areas: Migration, Community Model, Data Rights, Governance.

## 8. End-to-End Workflows

These are elaborated with packet overlays in
[Architecture V2 / 01](../Architecture%20V2/01-overall-system-architecture.md) and inventoried in
[Architecture V2 / 02](../Architecture%20V2/02-workflow-inventory-and-function-map.md).

1. **Build & publish** — owner registers (App ID) → logs in (Passport) → creates Community (handle) →
   Skill generates package → Loom validates + certifies → publish returns QR.
2. **Discover & install** — member adds community by handle/QR → extension downloads → approve
   permissions → card appears → open fetches latest version.
3. **Headline vertical flow** — member runs the vertical's core action (book vote / soccer
   registration / HOA architectural review / mosque care request) → rule/workflow fires → audit/receipt.
4. **Payments** — dues/donation intent → confirm → receipt → rule updates status.
5. **Messaging & in-stream ads** — member opens Messages → stream renders rich items → Loom injects an
   ad item → impression receipt.
6. **Connections & invitation** — member views Connections → invites a connection (unless blocked) →
   join request created.
7. **Ad-off** — member subscription or owner payment → ads suppressed → receipt.
8. **Export & migration** — owner requests plan → export → provider cutover → migration receipt.

## 9. Cross-Area Requirements

Every V2 product definition should answer:

- Does it preserve community ownership and portability?
- Does it preserve one-Passport member identity and member data control?
- Does it respect the effective-permission intersection and protected vaults for sensitive data?
- Does it keep the trust boundary (no raw DB access; mutations via Loom APIs; idempotent; audited)?
- Does it preserve the required shell structure (top ad banner; nav panel with Messages/Connections)?
- Does it keep ads un-removable (shell surfaces + in-stream items) and honor ad-off?
- Does it generate signed receipts for payments and ad events?
- Does it support export, migration, uninstall (jobs/rules disabled immediately), and disputes?
- Does it keep the Skill as builder, not runtime authority, with validation/certification before install?
- Does it remain backward-compatible with V1 channel/fan surfaces?

## 11. FAQ

### What new systems are needed versus reused from V1?

New: the community graph (communities/spaces/membership/roles), the extension platform engines (rules,
workflows, jobs, sandboxed functions, event bus, data schemas), the App Shell (cards/routes/surfaces/
nav panel + shells), the ads delivery (shell surfaces + in-stream injection), and the protected vaults.
Reused: Passport identity, Wallet, Receipt/Settlement, AI Gateway, migration/export, and the
contract-first demo harness. See the
[reuse map](./Extensible%20Loom%20Product%20Definition.md#2-what-carries-over-from-loom-v1-reuse-map).

### Why is the Skill not the runtime?

ChatGPT, Gemini, and Claude have different skill/app models; depending on one at runtime re-introduces
lock-in. The Skill compiles a portable Loom package that Loom runs.

### How are ads protected from being "designed out"?

Shell ad surfaces are Loom-rendered and un-removable; in-stream ads are injected as native API stream
items the extension can't suppress. Ad integrity is a certification check.
