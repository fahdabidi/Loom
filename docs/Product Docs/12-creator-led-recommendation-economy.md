## 1. Product Definition

The Creator-Led Recommendation Economy makes creator trust a first-class discovery system. Instead of relying only on opaque engagement-maximizing feeds, creators can publish portable `RecommendationManifest` records that fan apps rank according to fan preferences. Destination creators can publish referral terms, and qualified engagement can generate referral revenue.

The goal is not to remove algorithmic ranking. The goal is to make the source of recommendation candidates more accountable, portable, and economically transparent.

Recommendation providers and fan apps can rank eligible candidates, but they cannot become owners of the discovery graph, broad autonomous crawlers, undisclosed paid-ranking feeds, or substitutes for neutral public search.

Loom recommendations are organized around two distinct fan-controlled concepts:

- `PlatformIntent`: a platform-defined reason for using Loom right now, such as learning, browsing with low engagement, shopping, reviews/research, entertainment, catching up with friends and family, catching up with followed creators, search, calm/wind-down, or news/briefing. The platform intent sets the engagement posture, creator-vs-provider recommendation blend, ad posture, and clickbait/ragebait tolerance.
- `FanInterestProfile`: a fan-owned vault profile of interests and dislikes, such as tennis, cricket, comedy, Marvel movies, disliked topics, disliked creators, muted creators, and feedback-derived preference signals. It is derived from explicit startup tile selections, followed creators, likes/dislikes, saves, search, watch/read behavior where allowed, and follow/unfollow actions.

At app launch, the Fan App presents content tiles generated by combining platform-defined intents with the fan's interests and dislikes. A tile might mean "Learn about tennis", "Low-engagement browsing for comedy", "Reviews for video games", "Catch up with friends and family", or "Entertainment around Marvel movies." When the fan clicks a tile, Loom creates a `SessionIntent` that captures the chosen `PlatformIntent` plus scoped interest tokens and dislike filters for that session.

`RecommendationModePolicy` remains the machine-readable enforcement layer underneath the platform intent. It transparently sets ranking objective, data posture, creator/provider blend, ad load and creator breadth, and session shape. Fan interests decide what the content is about; platform intent decides why the fan is here and how aggressive ranking, ads, and external recommendation providers may be.

## 4. Product Experience Requirements

### 4.1 Creator Experience

Creators should be able to:

- Discover relevant creators and content.
- Review reputation, public metrics, topic fit, and referral terms.
- Draft recommendations with required disclosures.
- Publish, update, pause, or remove recommendations.
- See recommendation performance and referral revenue.
- Understand reputation effects.

### 4.2 Fan Experience

Fans should be able to:

- Choose a startup content tile with a platform intent and optional active interests at app launch.
- Switch or clear `SessionIntent` mid-session without losing portable state.
- See the platform intent objective, creator/provider blend, data posture, ad load/breadth, and session behavior before ranking begins.
- See recommendations from creators they trust.
- Understand why a recommendation is shown.
- Filter or mute recommendation sources, interests, creators, and external recommendation providers.
- Add optional community feeds.
- Like, dislike, flag, save, follow, unfollow, mute, or block content/creators.
- Avoid paid ranking disguised as search.

### 4.3 Destination Creator Experience

Destination creators should be able to:

- Publish referral terms.
- Limit eligibility, payout windows, caps, and abuse rules.
- See inbound recommendation performance.
- Dispute invalid referrals.

### 4.4 Platform Intents, Fan Interests, And Session Intent

Platform intents are predefined by Loom and certified in conformance tests. Apps can label them in their own UX, but cannot silently change their policy effect.

| Platform intent | Purpose | Creator/provider blend | Ad posture | Session shape |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Creator updates | Catch up with followed creators and memberships. | Strict followed creators first; creator recommendations allowed; external provider candidates minimal or disabled. | Low load; creator-approved and first-party inventory only. | Resume and catch-up. |
| Friends and family | Catch up with direct social connections. | Direct connections only unless the fan explicitly expands scope. | No behavioral ads; ads disabled or certified family-safe contextual only. | Strict direct-connection session. |
| Learning | Learn a subject or continue a course/deep dive. | Followed/trusted creators first; creator recommendations allowed; external provider candidates limited to high-reputation sources. | Minimal creator-approved contextual ads or sponsor offers. | Long-session, progress-aware. |
| Reviews / research | Evaluate products, games, media, or services with low engagement pressure. | Followed/trusted creators and reputable review sources first; external provider candidates limited and labeled. | Creator-approved contextual ads only; no urgency/scarcity formats. | Task-based comparison. |
| Shopping | Explore creator-approved offers, product cards, commerce, and affiliate content. | Creator-approved commerce and sponsor surfaces first; provider candidates must be commerce-certified and labeled. | Creator-approved commerce/sponsor inventory; explicit disclosure. | Task-based conversion. |
| Entertainment | Relax with higher novelty and broader content. | Followed/trusted creators, creator recommendations, community feeds, and certified external recommendation providers allowed within fan controls. | Moderate or higher contextual load where creator policy allows. | Lean-back. |
| Low-engagement browsing | Browse without high-stimulation/ragebait optimization. | Followed/trusted creators and calm community feeds; external provider candidates capped. | Low load; no high-stimulation formats. | Bounded, easy exit. |
| Search | Find a specific thing intentionally. | Neutral public search; not recommendation ranking. | No ads in search results. | Ask-first. |
| News / briefing | Catch up on timely events. | Followed/subscribed sources, source-diverse creator recommendations, high-reputation providers where allowed. | Contextual ads only where news safety rules and creator policy allow. | Briefing with source comparison. |

Fan interests are separate from platform intent. Interest tokens can be explicit, inferred, or negative. Examples include tennis, cricket, Marvel movies, video game reviews, creator ids, disliked topics, and disliked creators. Interest/dislike data lives in the Fan Vault and is evaluated by the Audience Data Firewall before being used outside the vault.

At startup, `StartupTileSurfaceAPI` combines platform intents with `FanInterestProfile` to generate tiles. A tile has one platform intent and zero or more interest tokens or dislike filters. Selecting a tile creates a `SessionIntent` with:

- `platformIntentId`: the session motive.
- `activeInterestTokens`: topics or creators the fan is choosing for this session.
- `activeDislikeFilters`: disliked topics, creators, formats, or providers to suppress.
- `creatorProviderBlend`: maximum candidate share from followed creators, creator recommendations, community feeds, and certified external recommendation providers.
- `adPosture`: creator-approved only, contextual creator-approved, broader creator-approved, no ads, or commerce/sponsor context.
- `storagePreference`: session-only by default, saved only if the fan chooses.

### 4.5 Intent-Aware Scoring

The same video can score differently depending on platform intent and fan interests/dislikes. Candidate scoring should produce a `ContentScoreExplanation` that shows the highest-impact factors without exposing private raw data.

| Signal | Scoring use |
| --- | --- |
| Followed creator | Strong positive signal for Creator updates, Learning, Reviews, and Friends and Family where applicable. |
| Recommendation from followed creator | Strong positive signal, subject to source reputation and disclosure. |
| Creator reputation | Second-line defense against clickbait, ragebait, undisclosed sponsorship, referral abuse, and low-quality recommendations. |
| External recommendation provider score | Allowed only within the platform intent's provider quota and certification scope; must be labeled. |
| Host content performance metadata | Trending, being watched now, fresh, total view count, velocity, completion, and safety-filtered momentum from certified hosts. |
| Content summary relevance | Strong positive or negative relevance against `ContentManifest.summary`, especially when the fan or app asks an agent to ignore sensational titles. |
| Title risk / title-summary mismatch | Downweight title wording that appears clickbait/ragebait or materially mismatches the creator-approved summary; do not hide the title from the fan. |
| Interest relevance | Positive match against active fan interests for the session. |
| Disliked interest / disliked creator | Strong suppression or exclusion depending on fan setting. |
| Content feedback | Like, dislike, save, flag, not interested, and watch/read completion update future interest/dislike tokens. |
| Safety and moderation labels | Suppress or exclude content that violates platform intent, age/region policy, or safety constraints. |

Platform intent is the first-line protection against clickbait and ragebait because it constrains how much algorithmic/external content can enter the candidate set. Creator reputation is the second defense because even allowed candidates are suppressed when creators or providers build a pattern of abusive recommendations.

Fan-controlled agents may add a session instruction such as "ignore ragebait/clickbait titles and rank from summaries." This does not expand the candidate set for Fan Recommendation AI: it still ranks only eligible content from followed creators, trusted creators, and creator-authored recommendations. The instruction changes metadata weighting by prioritizing `ContentManifest.summary` over title wording and recording `summaryUsedForRelevance` / `titleDeemphasized` in the score explanation where useful.

## 5. User Stories

### Story 1: Creator recommends trusted creator

As a creator, I want to recommend another creator I trust so my fans can discover relevant work.

End state:

- `RecommendationManifest` is published.
- Fan apps can show recommendation.
- Disclosure is visible.

### Story 2: Creator earns referral revenue

As a creator, I want referral revenue when my recommendation drives qualified engagement.

End state:

- Destination creator has referral terms.
- Qualified action creates receipt.
- Settlement allocates referral revenue.

### Story 3: Fan controls recommendation sources

As a fan, I want to choose which creator recommendations influence my feed.

End state:

- Fan settings control sources.
- Fan app ranks candidates.
- Feedback affects future ranking.

### Story 3A: Fan chooses platform intent and interest from startup tiles

As a fan, I want Loom to show content tiles based on platform-defined intents and my interests/dislikes so I can choose why I am here and what I want to see without being dropped into an engagement-max feed.

End state:

- Fan selects a `ContentTile` that has a `PlatformIntent` and scoped interest/dislike tokens.
- `SessionIntentDisclosure` shows purpose, creator/provider blend, data posture, ad posture, algorithmic breadth, and session shape.
- `RecommendationModePolicy` constrains ranking, data access, ads, external recommendation providers, and session defaults.
- Interest/dislike updates are stored in Fan Vault only according to fan policy.

### Story 4: Destination creator sets terms

As a destination creator, I want to define referral payout terms so discovery economics are predictable.

End state:

- `ReferralTermsManifest` is published.
- Terms include action types, windows, caps, and exclusions.

### Story 5: Governance handles recommendation abuse

As governance, I want to detect clickbait and fake referral loops.

End state:

- Abuse signals are recorded.
- Recommendation reputation updates.
- Settlement can apply fraud adjustments.

### Story 6: Creator uses AI workbench

As a creator, I want AI help finding creators to recommend, but I want final editorial control.

End state:

- AI drafts candidates using bounded tools.
- Creator reviews disclosures.
- Creator publishes recommendation.

### Story 7: Host contributes trending statistics

As a hosting provider, I want to publish aggregate trending statistics for eligible public content so Trending and Good Time session intents can reflect real momentum without exposing raw fan behavior.

End state:

- `HostingTrendingStatsAPI` publishes aggregate velocity, novelty, geography/locale buckets where allowed, and eligibility metadata.
- `TrendingSignalReceipt` or audit log records source host, time window, and policy version.
- Ranking services can use the signal only for session intents that allow aggregate trending inputs.

### Story 8: Fan likes, dislikes, flags, follows, or unfollows

As a fan, I want normal controls like like, dislike, flag, not interested, follow, and unfollow so Loom can learn my interests and dislikes while still letting me correct the system.

End state:

- `FanContentFeedbackAPI` records the action.
- Fan Vault updates interest, disliked interest, liked creator, disliked creator, mute, or block state where appropriate.
- Trust and Safety receives flag actions.
- Future feed scoring suppresses disliked interests and creators and explains major preference effects.

### Story 9: Fan agent ignores clickbait titles

As a fan, I want my personal Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini, or other MCP-based recommendation agent to ignore clickbait or ragebait title wording and use creator-approved summaries to find relevant content.

End state:

- Agent receives only eligible followed-creator and creator-recommendation candidates allowed by the current Fan Recommendation AI definition.
- Candidate metadata includes title, `ContentManifest.summary`, creator, recommendation source, and disclosure state.
- Scoring can mark title wording as deemphasized and summary relevance as used.
- Fan sees normal titles and disclosures in the UI, plus a why-shown explanation if summary-first ranking changed the order.

## 6. End-to-End Workflows

### Workflow 1: Destination creator publishes referral terms

Actors:

- Destination Creator
- Creator Studio
- Creator Metadata Host
- Settlement Engine

Steps:

1. Destination creator opens referral settings.
2. Creator defines eligible actions, payout amount or percentage, attribution window, caps, exclusions, and fraud rules.
3. Creator Studio validates terms.
4. `ReferralTermsManifest` is stored in channel metadata.
5. Creator Discovery Exchange can surface terms.

### Workflow 2: Source creator publishes recommendation

Actors:

- Source Creator
- Creator Discovery Exchange
- Recommendation Workbench
- AI Gateway
- Creator Metadata Host

Steps:

1. Source creator searches for creators, content, promotions, and referral terms.
2. Creator reviews reputation and fit.
3. Creator optionally grants `CreatorAgentDelegationToken` access to `RecommendationWorkbenchMCPServer` with bounded research and draft scopes.
4. AI-assisted draft records candidate provenance, sources, referral terms reviewed, and tool-call audit records.
5. Recommendation Workbench validates disclosure requirements, relationship labels, and referral-term compatibility.
6. Creator publishes `RecommendationManifest` through `RecommendationManifestAPI`.
7. Manifest becomes available to fan apps without transferring graph ownership to an app or provider.

### Workflow 3: Fan receives trusted recommendation

Actors:

- Fan
- Fan App
- Fan Scoped Recommendation Engine
- Creator Recommendation Graph
- Private Event Vault

Steps:

1. Fan App shows startup tiles generated from platform intents and the fan's `FanInterestProfile`.
2. Fan selects a tile; `SessionIntentAPI` creates a `SessionIntent` with `platformIntentId`, active interest tokens, dislike filters, creator/provider blend, and ad posture.
3. `SessionIntentDisclosure` shows ranking purpose, data posture, creator/provider blend, ad posture, and session shape.
4. Fan App fetches eligible candidates from followed creators, creator recommendations, direct connections, community feeds, fan-initiated search results, certified external recommendation providers, and `HostingTrendingStatsAPI` only as allowed by the platform intent.
5. `FanScopedRecommendationAPI` applies intent weights, `FanInterestProfile`, source filters, disclosure preferences, disliked interests, disliked creators, and trusted-candidate rules.
6. If private behavior influences ranking, `PrivateRankingAPI` runs in the Private Event Vault after a purpose-bound `DataUseGrant`; no raw private event data leaves the vault.
7. `DataAccessReceipt` records grant-protected private ranking access.
8. `ContentScoringService` scores candidates using followed-creator affinity, creator recommendations, external provider score, host content performance metadata, `ContentManifest.summary` relevance, title-risk/title-summary mismatch penalties, creator reputation, interest relevance, dislike penalties, safety labels, freshness, and trending momentum.
9. `SessionIntentAdContext` sets ad posture and creator/provider breadth; `CreatorAdPolicy` and sponsor policy decide which ads can actually appear.
10. App displays content tile/feed item with title, summary where appropriate, intent label, source, disclosure, score explanation, funding/referral label, and why-shown context.
11. Fan likes, dislikes, flags, follows, unfollows, saves, dismisses, mutes, blocks, clears intent, or switches intent.
12. `FanContentFeedbackAPI` updates Fan Vault interest/dislike state and future ranking.
13. Qualified discovery generates `DiscoveryReceipt`; eligible conversion may later generate `CreatorReferralReceipt`.

### Workflow 3A: Startup tile selection and mid-session switch

Actors:

- Fan
- Fan App
- Fan Scoped Recommendation Engine
- Audience Data Firewall
- Ad Decision System

Steps:

1. Fan opens app.
2. `StartupTileSurfaceAPI` reads platform-defined intents and the fan's allowed `FanInterestProfile`.
3. App displays tiles such as Learn: Tennis, Reviews: Video Games, Entertainment: Comedy, Creator Updates, or Friends and Family.
4. Fan selects a tile, searches for a topic, mutes an interest, or starts from Creator Updates.
5. `SessionIntentAPI` sends `platformIntentId`, active interest tokens, dislike filters, creator/provider blend, ad posture, and storage preference to `FanScopedRecommendationAPI`.
6. `AudienceDataFirewallPolicy` checks whether the session's requested interest/dislike posture is allowed by privacy mode, grants, age rules, and vault policy.
7. `SessionIntentAdContext` sends intent-level ad posture, load, creator/provider breadth, and creator-approved-only constraints to the ad decision boundary.
8. Ranking begins with intent-specific objective, candidate quotas, and score weights.
9. If the fan switches intent mid-session, the engine re-ranks from the new policy and records a new session intent context for future receipts.
10. Fan can save the preference, use it for this session only, dislike the interest, dislike the creator, mute the provider, or clear the intent.

### Workflow 4: Referral settlement

Actors:

- Fan
- Source Creator
- Destination Creator
- Receipt Ledger
- Settlement Engine

Steps:

1. Fan engages with destination creator after recommendation.
2. Attribution rules evaluate eligibility against the effective `RecommendationManifest`, `ReferralTermsManifest`, attribution window, fan privacy mode, and `SettlementManifest`.
3. `DiscoveryReceipt` or `CreatorReferralReceipt` is signed by the responsible app/provider and submitted through `ReceiptIngestAPI`.
4. `ReceiptLedger` stores immutable receipt evidence and manifest-version bindings.
5. Fraud signals attach confidence scores, invalid-referral flags, or `FraudAdjustmentRecord` entries.
6. `SettlementEngineAPI` applies referral terms, caps, reserves, chargebacks, and dispute adjustments.
7. `SettlementRunRecord` and `CreatorPayoutStatement` show source creator revenue, destination creator cost, and provider adjustments.

### Workflow 5: Community feed subscription

Actors:

- Fan
- Fan App
- Community Recommendation Feed Provider
- Governance System

Steps:

1. Fan browses available community feeds.
2. App shows feed topic, `CommunityRecommendationProviderManifest`, curation policy, funding model, ranking policy, certification state, and governance information.
3. Fan subscribes to feed.
4. `CommunityFeedAPI` provides recommendation candidates only from declared eligible sources.
5. Fan Scoped Recommendation Engine ranks them with fan settings and disclosure constraints.
6. `CommunityFeedAuditAPI` records feed delivery, funding disclosure, and ranking-policy compliance.
7. Community feed providers cannot sell paid-search placement or per-click ranking by default.
8. Fan can unsubscribe.

### Workflow 6: Recommendation abuse review

Actors:

- Fan
- Creator
- Fraud Signal Service
- Governance Admin
- Settlement Engine

Steps:

1. Abuse signals identify suspicious recommendations.
2. Signals may include clickbait, undisclosed paid relationships, referral loops, fake engagement, or high bounce.
3. Governance reviews evidence.
4. Recommendation reputation is adjusted.
5. Referral settlement may be held or adjusted.
6. Repeat abuse can affect creator or provider trust status.

### Workflow 7: Hosting trending statistics ingestion

Actors:

- Hosting Provider
- Content Host
- Fan Scoped Recommendation Engine
- Governance/Audit System

Steps:

1. Certified hosting provider aggregates eligible public content velocity, novelty, completion, freshness, being-watched-now state, total view count, and safety-filtered momentum over a declared time window.
2. Host excludes private-mode fan behavior, non-consented private vault signals, minors' restricted signals, and content not eligible for trending exposure.
3. Host signs a `HostingTrendingStatsBatch` with provider id, API version, time window, aggregation thresholds, content references, and public content performance fields.
4. `HostingTrendingStatsAPI` publishes the batch to recommendation services.
5. Recommendation services use the batch only for session intents whose `RecommendationModePolicy` permits aggregate public content performance signals.
6. Governance can audit host trending claims through `TrendingSignalAuditAPI`, receipt samples, and replayable aggregation evidence.

## 7. Cross-Area Interactions

- Creator Experience: creators build and manage `RecommendationManifest`, `ReferralTermsManifest`, disclosures, and performance reporting.
- Fan Experience: fans consume and control recommendations through `FanRecommendationSettings`, `FanInterestProfile`, `FanScopedRecommendationAPI`, and `FanContentFeedbackAPI`.
- Fan Apps and App Ecosystem: apps must expose startup content tile surfaces, session intent disclosure, mid-session switching, and policy-safe session behavior.
- Fan Passport, Wallet, Vaults, and Identity Architecture: fan settings, `DataUseGrant`, and `PrivateRankingAPI` are enforced through vaults.
- Provider Marketplace and Certified APIs: community feed and recommendation providers require `ProviderCertificationAPI` and `CertificationScopeRecord`.
- Neutral Public Search Utility: `SearchReceipt`, `SearchDirectoryAPI`, and `NeutralSearchMergePolicy` remain separate from recommendations.
- Creator Channel and Metadata Architecture: `ContentManifest.summary` is required beside title and gives recommendation engines and fan agents creator-approved relevance metadata.
- Hosting Provider Lifecycle and Progressive Unbundling: `HostingTrendingStatsAPI` provides aggregate public momentum signals for eligible session intents.
- Revenue, Receipts, Ledgers, and Settlement: `DiscoveryReceipt`, `CreatorReferralReceipt`, and `SettlementManifest` drive referral payouts.
- AI Layer: `RecommendationWorkbenchMCPServer`, `CreatorAgentDelegationToken`, and audit logs support recommendation drafting; `FanRecommendationMCPServer` can use summaries and title-deemphasis instructions without expanding candidate sources.
- Audience Data Firewall and Data Rights: private ranking requires `DataUseGrant`, `PrivateRankingAPI`, and `DataAccessReceipt`.
- Brand/Sponsor/Advertiser Tools: platform intent sets ad load/breadth, creator-approved-only posture, and contextual ad category, but creator ad policy and sponsor policy decide eligible ads and disclosures.
- Trust, Safety, Fraud, and Compliance: `RecommendationAbuseAPI`, disclosure audits, and `FraudAdjustmentRecord` enforce recommendation trust.
- Governance, Certification, and Foundation Model: `CertificationScopeRecord`, `DisputeResolutionAPI`, and reputation policy govern enforcement.
- Business Model and Incentive Design: `ReferralTermsManifest`, caps, reserves, and incentive audits avoid predatory engagement loops.

## 8. FAQ

### What key systems and/or capabilities need to be created/implemented or modified to implement the key features, user stories, and end-to-end workflows?

#### Creator discovery systems

- `CreatorDiscoveryAPI`: broad discovery for creators, content, events, promotions, and public profiles.
- `PromotionDiscoveryAPI`: campaigns, giveaways, events, launches, and offers.
- `ReferralTermsDiscoveryAPI`: creators with active referral terms.
- `CreatorReputationAPI`: quality, trust, recommendation reputation, abuse signals, and public metrics.
- `CreatorAgentDelegationToken`: bounded AI access for creator recommendation research.
- `RecommendationWorkbenchMCPServer`: scoped AI tool surface for recommendation research and draft generation.

#### Recommendation authoring systems

- `RecommendationWorkbenchAPI`: draft, validate, label, publish, update, pause, and delete recommendations.
- `RecommendationManifestAPI`: publishing and lifecycle API for creator recommendations.
- `RecommendationManifest`: source creator, destination creator/content, reason, disclosure, ranking hints, expiration, and labels.
- `RecommendationDisclosurePolicy`: editorial, paid, affiliate, sponsored, referral, or other relationship labels.
- `RecommendationWorkbenchAuditLog`: candidate source provenance, tool calls, disclosure checks, and publishing events.

#### Fan recommendation systems

- `PlatformIntentRegistry`: certified list of allowed session motives and policy effects.
- `PlatformIntent`: predefined intent object with purpose, engagement posture, creator/provider blend, ad posture, and session shape.
- `FanInterestProfile`: fan-owned vault profile of interest tokens, disliked interests, liked/disliked creators, muted creators, and confidence/source metadata.
- `StartupTileSurfaceAPI`: returns launch and mid-session content tiles by combining platform intents with allowed fan interests and dislikes.
- `ContentTile`: fan-facing tile with platform intent, active interest tokens, dislike filters, source explanation, and disclosure summary.
- `SessionIntentAPI`: creates, switches, clears, and optionally saves the fan's current platform intent plus scoped interest context.
- `SessionIntent`: session-scoped object with `platformIntentId`, active interest tokens, active dislike filters, creator/provider blend, ad posture, score weights, session shape, and disclosure.
- `SessionIntentDisclosure`: fan-facing disclosure of purpose, creator/provider blend, ad posture, data use, and tradeoffs.
- `SessionIntentAdContext`: ad posture, contextual category, creator-approved-only flag, ad-load/breadth constraints, and no-raw-behavior boundary passed to ad decision systems.
- `FanScopedRecommendationAPI`: trusted candidate set, fan interest profile, session intent, source filters, scoring weights, and feedback.
- `ContentManifest.summary`: required creator-approved content summary supplied beside title for recommendation relevance, fan agents, search snippets, and accessibility surfaces.
- `RecommendationMetadataPreference`: fan/app/agent instruction object for using summaries and deemphasizing title wording when title risk is detected.
- `FanRecommendationSettings`: source controls, interest preferences, disliked interests, disliked creators, muted providers, community feeds, privacy mode, and disclosure preferences.
- `ContentScoringService`: scores content per platform intent and fan interest/dislike context.
- `ContentScoreExplanation`: fan-facing why-shown summary with major score factors and disclosure constraints.
- `RecommendationMode`: policy envelope object with objective, data posture, ad load/breadth, and session shape.
- `RecommendationModePolicy`: machine-readable constraints for each mode.
- `RecommendationModeSelector`: compatibility API surface for certified apps that expose explicit mode controls instead of seed-first controls.
- `ModeDisclosureCard`: compatibility disclosure of the mode objective and tradeoffs.
- `RankingObjectivePolicy`: mode-specific ranking goal such as creator trust, trending velocity, intent, briefing, learning, calm, direct connections, or good-time entertainment.
- `RecommendationDataPosturePolicy`: mode-specific allowed data classes, bounded by fan privacy mode and grants.
- `AdLoadPolicy`: mode-specific ad load and eligible creator breadth.
- `CreatorAdPolicy`: creator-controlled ad eligibility; mode policy cannot override creator ad policy.
- `SessionShapePolicy`: mode defaults for resume, ask-first, lean-back, briefing, bounded, or family-safe session behavior.
- `PrivateRankingAPI`: in-vault ranking where privacy mode or private behavior requires it.
- `DataUseGrant`: purpose-bound authorization for private ranking.
- `DataAccessReceipt`: audit record for grant-protected private ranking access.
- `FanContentFeedbackAPI`: like, dislike, not interested, flag, save, follow, unfollow, mute creator, block creator, and provider mute actions.
- `FanInterestSignalProcessor`: updates interest and dislike tokens from explicit tile selection, follows, unfollows, likes, dislikes, flags, saves, and allowed behavior.
- `CommunityFeedSubscriptionAPI`: opt in/out of community recommendation feeds.
- `CommunityFeedAPI`: curated feed candidate delivery from declared eligible sources.
- `CommunityRecommendationProviderManifest`: topic, funding, ranking, data-use, and governance policy for community feeds.
- `CommunityFeedAuditAPI`: delivery, funding disclosure, ranking-policy, and abuse audit records.
- `HostingTrendingStatsAPI`: host-published aggregate trending velocity, novelty, freshness, being-watched-now, total view count, completion, and momentum signals for eligible public content.
- `HostingTrendingStatsBatch`: signed aggregate batch with source host, time window, thresholds, eligible content refs, performance fields, and policy version.
- `TrendingSignalAuditAPI`: governance audit surface for host trending statistics.

#### Referral and settlement systems

- `ReferralTermsManifest`: payout terms, eligible actions, attribution windows, caps, fraud rules, and exclusions.
- `CreatorReferralReceipt`: qualified referral event.
- `DiscoveryReceipt`: recommendation-driven discovery event.
- `ReceiptIngestAPI`: receipt validation and ledger submission.
- `ReceiptLedger`: immutable receipt store with manifest-version bindings.
- `SettlementManifest`: economic routing and settlement policy.
- `SettlementEngineAPI`: applies referral terms to settlement.
- `SettlementRunRecord`: settlement run output with referral allocations and adjustments.
- `CreatorPayoutStatement`: creator-facing statement for referral revenue or costs.
- `FraudAdjustmentRecord`: invalid referral or fake engagement adjustments.

#### Trust and governance systems

- `RecommendationReputationAPI`: source quality, fan feedback, clickbait signals, conversion quality, and abuse history.
- `RecommendationAbuseAPI`: reports and signals for spam, undisclosed paid placement, fake referrals, and manipulation.
- `DisclosureAuditAPI`: checks labels and paid relationships.
- `DisputeResolutionAPI`: referral and recommendation disputes.
- `CertificationScopeRecord`: provider/app/community-feed certification boundary.
